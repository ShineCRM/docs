title: "Message rewriter"
description: "On-demand polish for SMS and email drafts before you send them."
---

The message rewriter is the "polish my draft" button inside customer conversations. You type a rough draft, tap the rewrite action, and the AI returns a cleaner version tuned to the channel.

## When to use it

Use it when you have a draft already typed and you want the AI to fix tone, grammar, or length without inventing new content. The rewriter never adds new information that wasn't already in your draft.

## How it adapts to channel

The rewriter checks whether the conversation is SMS or email and applies different rules:

**SMS:**
- Tight, ideally under 320 characters
- One or two short paragraphs at most
- No formal greeting or sign-off
- Conversational, first-name basis

**Email:**
- Paragraphs allowed for readability
- Natural greeting and sign-off only if your original had one
- No subject line (your subject is handled separately)
- No signature block (the app appends one)

## What gets preserved

The rewriter is instructed to **preserve facts exactly**, including:

- Prices, dates, addresses, names
- Links (review request links, payment links, etc.)
- The original intent and meaning of the message

It only fixes tone, grammar, awkward phrasing, and typos.

## Extra instructions

You can pass an optional instruction along with the rewrite (e.g. "make it warmer," "shorter," "more apologetic"). The AI tries to honor it without altering facts.

## What it does not do

- Add information that wasn't in your draft
- Add a subject line for emails
- Add a signature block
- Wrap the output in quotes or commentary (it returns the rewritten body only)
- Translate to another language (unless you ask in your extra instruction)

## Cost

The rewriter uses a fast, low-cost model by default. Per-call cost is typically under a fraction of a cent.
